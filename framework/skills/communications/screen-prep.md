# Skill: Screen & Call Prep

**Load when:** any phone screen, recruiter call, or warm-contact call is
scheduled or might happen mid-session.

**Depends on:** the private instance's per-company brief files (state), its
verified experience summary (claims), and its tracker (status updates after).

---

## The problem

Top-of-funnel work systematizes well; calls don't feel like they do — so they
tend to get improvised. But calls are where offers actually come from, and
the inputs to a good call are exactly the kind of scattered state (who said
what, which role has which gate, what gap to name first) that this system
already tracks. The fix is a per-company brief: one glanceable document per
live company, built to be read mid-call on a phone.

## The company brief

One file per company with a live thread, in the private data layer. It must
answer, at a glance:

1. **Contacts and history** — every prior touch, who introduced whom, what
   each person has already offered or promised.
2. **Roles in play** — each with its status and its gate (comp under floor,
   a hard requirement line, location ambiguity).
3. **Gaps to name proactively**, with the honest reframe from the verified
   summary. Naming a gap before they do converts it from a discovery into a
   data point.
4. **The one story to land** — picked for this company's problem, not the
   generic best-of reel.
5. **The candidate's questions** — including any hard gating question that
   decides whether to keep investing. Ask it early, not last.

## During the call

- Comp posture: state the floor plainly when asked. If the posted band is
  under it, tie the gap to scope rather than accepting silently.
- Claims discipline holds out loud: nothing said on a call that isn't
  defensible from the verified experience summary.

## After (same day, not later)

Update the brief (what changed, what was promised, by whom, by when); log
the outcome; update the tracker if a status moved; promote anything reusable
(a comp data point, a framing that landed) to the right durable file.
