## [Employer One] — [Title], [Start] – [End]

For each employer, write the verified account: what you actually did, in
plain language, before any resume polish. Include:

- What the team/system/product actually was, not the impressive-sounding
  version.
- What you specifically did versus what the team or a predecessor did. This
  distinction matters more than it seems like it should — it's the single
  most common place overclaims creep in.
- Concrete numbers where you actually have them (scale, duration, team
  size). If you don't have a real number, don't invent one — say what you
  do know.
- Anything that's true but easy to accidentally overstate. Write the
  corrected version here, not just the polished one.

If a role had genuinely distinct phases or teams, break them out as
subsections rather than blending them into one narrative.

## Overclaims to avoid

**This section is as load-bearing as the verified facts above it.** Every
time a draft of a resume, cover letter, or message rounds a fact up, write
down the overclaim and the corrected version here, and don't let it
reappear later. A rejected exaggeration that isn't written down anywhere
can quietly resurface in a session that doesn't have the earlier
conversation in context.

Format: `Never claim "[the overclaim]" — the accurate version is [correction].`
